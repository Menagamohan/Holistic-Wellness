Robotic Spinal Decompression Therapy

Robotic Spinal Decompression Therapy is a non-surgical, computer-controlled treatment designed to relieve back and neck pain by gently stretching the spine. Using advanced robotic technology, the system precisely targets affected spinal discs to reduce pressure, promote healing, and restore mobility. This therapy is commonly used to treat conditions such as herniated discs, bulging discs, sciatica, degenerative disc disease, and chronic lower back or neck pain.
VIEW MORE

How It Works

The robotic system uses computerized sensors to:

  • Analyze spinal alignment
  • Detect muscle resistance
  • Apply controlled decompression forces
  • Adjust in real-time for maximum comfort and effectiveness

By creating negative pressure within the spinal disc, decompression:

  • Encourages retraction of bulging or herniated discs
  • Improves blood flow and nutrient exchange
  • Reduces nerve compression
  • Promotes natural healing

Unlike manual traction, robotic decompression offers precise, repeatable, and customized treatment for each patient.

Conditions Treated

Robotic spinal decompression therapy may help with:

  • Herniated or bulging discs
  • Sciatica
  • Degenerative disc disease
  • Facet joint syndrome
  • Pinched nerves
  • Chronic neck or lower back pain
  • Post-surgical back pain (in selected cases)
