Advanced IV Nutrition Therapy

Advanced IV Nutrition Therapy (also called intravenous micronutrient therapy) is a medical treatment that delivers vitamins, minerals, amino acids, antioxidants, and other nutrients **directly into the bloodstream** through an IV (intravenous) line. Because it bypasses the digestive system, it allows for higher absorption and rapid delivery compared to oral supplements.
Advanced IV Nutrition Therapy is a medical treatment offered in clinics, hospitals, integrative or functional medicine centers, wellness clinics, and sometimes through concierge or mobile medical services. It is administered by licensed healthcare professionals such as registered nurses (RN), nurse practitioners (NP), medical doctors (MD), or physician assistants (PA) to ensure safety and proper supervision.

Common Nutrients Used

Advanced IV Nutrition Therapy typically includes a combination of vitamins, minerals, amino acids, antioxidants, and other supportive nutrients. Common vitamins such as Vitamin C, B-complex, B12, B6, and Biotin help support energy production and immune health. Minerals like magnesium, calcium, zinc, and selenium contribute to muscle function, bone health, and cellular balance. Amino acids including glutamine, arginine, taurine, and carnitine assist in tissue repair and metabolism. Antioxidants such as glutathione and alpha-lipoic acid help combat oxidative stress, while additional components like electrolytes, NAD+, trace minerals, and immune-support blends enhance hydration, recovery, and overall wellness.

Types of IV Therapy Formulations

Myers’ Cocktail, originally developed by John Myers, is a well-known IV formulation containing magnesium, calcium, B vitamins, and vitamin C. It is commonly used to support fatigue, migraines, asthma, and fibromyalgia.

High-dose Vitamin C therapy is used in integrative medicine, particularly for immune support and as a complementary approach in oncology. It has been studied by institutions such as the National Institutes of Health for potential supportive roles in cancer care.

NAD+ therapy delivers nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide intravenously to support cellular energy production. It is often marketed for anti-aging benefits, brain performance, and addiction recovery support.

Immune boost drips are commonly used during cold and flu season and typically contain vitamin C, zinc, glutathione, and B-complex vitamins to support immune function.

Athletic recovery drips are popular among athletes and may include amino acids, magnesium, electrolytes, and B vitamins to promote muscle recovery and hydration.
